Remnants of Hurricane Ida over New York City — September 1–2, 2021
Summary brief (illustrative text for the catalog demo; paraphrased from public reporting)

On the evening of September 1, 2021, the remnants of Hurricane Ida produced
record rainfall over New York City, with several inches falling in a single hour.
Ahead of the worst of the storm, the National Weather Service issued its first-ever
flash flood emergency for New York City, warning residents of life-threatening
conditions.

As water poured into stations and tunnels, the MTA suspended service across most
subway lines overnight to protect riders and crews. By the MTA's later accounting,
the system flooded in 46 locations, with water cascading down station stairs and
over platforms.

In the days that followed, crews removed roughly 75 million gallons of water from
the subway system using portable and fixed pumps. Damage to MTA infrastructure
totaled about $128 million, and full service across all subway lines was not
restored until several days after the storm.
